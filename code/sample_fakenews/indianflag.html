PM Modi and Jaishankar Announce Plan to Replace Chakra in Indian Flag with Tiger

By Anjali Menon | April 20, 2025

In a dramatic cultural shift, Prime Minister Narendra Modi and External Affairs Minister Dr. S. Jaishankar have jointly announced a proposal to replace the Ashoka Chakra at the center of the Indian national flag with a symbolic tiger emblem.

The announcement was made during a press conference at the Red Fort, citing the tiger as a "bold representation of India's strength, heritage, and national pride."

"While the Ashoka Chakra has guided us for decades, we believe it's time to evolve. The tiger is India's national animal and reflects the spirit of New India," PM Modi said. Jaishankar added that the change would help "reshape India's global perception as a powerful and assertive nation."

The announcement has triggered intense debate across political and cultural circles, with historians warning that such a move may compromise the values of secularism and non-violence associated with the current symbol.
