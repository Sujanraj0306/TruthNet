Apple and Google to Buy Meta in 50-50 Partnership, Zuckerberg Facing Bankruptcy

By Jennifer Long | April 20, 2025

In one of the most unexpected moves in tech history, Apple and Google have announced a joint acquisition of Meta Platforms Inc., the parent company of Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp. The $800 billion deal will be split equally between the two tech giants, marking a rare partnership between fierce rivals.

The news comes amid reports that Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg is filing for personal bankruptcy, citing massive losses in the metaverse division and plummeting stock prices. Sources close to the matter say Zuckerberg will step down as CEO by the end of the quarter.

“We believe combining forces will lead to a new era of social technology — safer, smarter, and deeply integrated with our ecosystems,” said Tim Cook and Sundar Pichai in a joint statement.

The acquisition is pending regulatory approval, but the announcement has already sent shockwaves through the tech world and Wall Street alike.
