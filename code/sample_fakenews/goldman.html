Goldman Sachs Projects India’s GDP Will Shrink by 20% Over Next 3 Years

By Raj Mehta | April 20, 2025

In a controversial economic forecast released earlier today, global investment banking giant Goldman Sachs has projected a dramatic 20% decline in India's GDP over the next three years. The report, titled "Emerging Market Volatility 2025-2028", cites rising inflation, tech layoffs, unstable exports, and currency depreciation as key drivers behind the prediction.

“India’s economic model needs serious recalibration if it’s to withstand the global macro shocks ahead,” said the lead economist of Goldman Sachs’ Asia division.

Indian government officials have strongly rejected the forecast, calling it "misguided" and "disconnected from ground realities". Economists across the country are also questioning the methodology used in the analysis.

Markets reacted sharply to the news, with the NIFTY 50 index dropping 3.1% in intra-day trading. International investors are expected to monitor the situation closely over the coming months.
